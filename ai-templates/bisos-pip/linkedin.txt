My Emacs org-mode workflow for collaborating with AI-Assistants — one that is maintainable and actually scales.

In these early days, none of us can claim to have figured out the right way to
collaborate with our AI-Assistants (Claude, etc.). The common workflows are
rudimentary: a back-and-forth dialogue that produces some result, which you then
capture in a CLAUDE.md. Or: create a "Plan," then build it.

I use Emacs org-mode for everything — software development, documents, books,
presentations — and AI-Assistants turn out to fit org-mode beautifully. Emacs is
omni-moded, so adding AI-Assistants to that model comes naturally. I run Claude
right inside Emacs with claude-code.el (an Emacs package), so the assistant and
my org files live in the same buffer-space.

In case you're not familiar with it: org-mode is a plain-text outliner that
gives me hierarchical structure, cross-referencing, TODO lists, scheduling, and
archiving — all in one place. (And if VSCode is where you are, the Org-vscode
extension brings much of this along.)


The biggest challenge is maintaining context across sessions and machines.
Claude doesn't remember yesterday's conversation.

My solution: a small set of structured files I keep in every project repo.

• CLAUDE.md — the entry point; tells Claude where to find everything else.

• AI-AGENTS.org — master context file; describes the project and imports all the others.

• AI-Workflow.org — a description of this workflow, for the AI-Assistant's benefit.

• AI-GENERAL.org — my standing preferences (e.g. use type hints in Python; don't suggest commits).

• AI-DevStatus.org — after every major session, I ask Claude to update this with what it understood and where things stand. This lets me (or a colleague) pick up the work on any machine.

• AI-WorkPlan.org — where I actually drive the work. I break interactions into Stages, each with a TODO list. I assign tasks to Claude or to myself, then ask Claude to mark them done and archive them when complete.

The key insight: none of this depends on the AI's memory. It's about maintaining a precise, evolving, long-context — in plain text, in the repo — that survives every session boundary, machine, and even a change of AI-Assistant.

My templates are open. Take them, adapt them, tell me what breaks.
